Why this model exists

The workflow is designed for policy and planning conversations where panel members need transparent evidence for what drives heat exposure and how it evolves over time.

Spatial diagnosis

Estimate monthly UHI intensity at sampled points across DFW to identify high-risk built environments and persistent cooling deficits.

Explanatory structure

Quantify how vegetation, built form, radiative surface properties, and atmospheric context contribute to UHI severity.

Forward planning

Forecast monthly aggregate UHI trajectory so the panel can discuss seasonal risk and intervention timing with a baseline comparison.

Pipeline at a glance

1. Data fusion
MODIS, VIIRS, ERA5-Land, NLCD, SRTM, and JRC assets are composited monthly in Google Earth Engine.
2. UHI target construction
`uhi_c = lst_day_c - rural_mean_c`, with robust fallback logic for rural reference computation.
3. Cleaning and feature engineering
DFW-only filtering, imputation, seasonality encodings, and spatial interaction terms.
4. Modeling and validation
Leakage-controlled ML (best: tuned XGBoost) plus ARIMA forecasting with seasonal-naive benchmarking.
Spatial UHI signal in DFW

Observed clustering motivated explicit spatial covariates (`lat`, `lon`, `lat2`, `lon2`, `lat_lon`) and strict in-domain filtering.
