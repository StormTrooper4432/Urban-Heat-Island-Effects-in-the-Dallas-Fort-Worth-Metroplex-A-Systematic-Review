Methods

Model design, assumptions, and data processing

This page captures the full technical chain used for the DFW UHI model: data reliability, preprocessing operations, variable semantics, mathematical formulations, and modeling assumptions.

Target definition and scope

Model domain is strictly Dallas-Fort Worth, Texas, enforced during sampling and preprocessing.

UHI target

uhi_c = lst_day_c - rural_mean_c, where the rural baseline is computed monthly from low-impervious and high-vegetation pixels. Fallback logic ensures no null target propagation.

Leakage policy: lst_day_c and rural_mean_c are excluded from model inputs because they are direct algebraic components of the target.

Model purpose

  • Estimate spatial variation in UHI severity across DFW.
  • Quantify variable influence and explanatory structure.
  • Forecast monthly city-scale UHI trajectory.

Data cleaning and preprocessing (complete audit)

Earth Engine transformations

  • Monthly compositing from 2016-01 through 2024-12.
  • LST conversion to Celsius (0.02 * raw - 273.15).
  • LST quality filter using QC bits 0-1 with threshold <=2.
  • NDVI/EVI scaling (* 0.0001), albedo scaling (* 0.001).
  • ERA5-Land temperature_2m converted to Celsius.
  • Terrain derivatives: slope and aspect from elevation.
  • Distance-to-water from JRC max extent mask.
  • Sample settings: numPixels=3000, scale=1000, seed=42, dropNulls=False, tileScale=4.

Tabular cleaning and model preprocessing

  • Drop rows with missing uhi_c.
  • Reinforce DFW-only bounds using latitude/longitude filters.
  • Mask out-of-range values to missing for LST, NDVI, EVI.
  • Median imputation for all numeric variables.
  • Landcover imputation to -1 and integer cast.
  • Derived features: is_urban, month_sin, month_cos, lat2, lon2, lat_lon.
  • Numeric standardization and one-hot encoding.
  • De-seasonalized residual training and seasonal reconstitution at inference.

Variable significance

Variable group Type Significance
Thermal continuous uhi_c is the target; night and atmospheric thermal terms capture residual heat behavior.
Vegetation and canopy continuous ndvi, evi, and tree_cover proxy evapotranspirative and shading effects.
Urban form continuous + binary + categorical impervious, is_urban, landcover, and night_lights encode built-environment intensity.
Radiative/topographic/hydrologic continuous albedo_wsa_sw, terrain variables, and dist_to_water_m capture energy balance and landscape context.
Spatiotemporal engineered continuous and ordinal Cyclical month features and spatial interactions model periodicity and nonlinear geographic gradients.

Model assumptions

  • DFW bounding box is an operationally adequate spatial approximation.
  • Monthly seasonality is stable enough for cyclical encoding and residualization.
  • Median imputation is acceptable under mixed-source missingness.
  • Predictor-target relationships are nonlinear.
  • SARIMA-type structure is valid for monthly aggregate forecasting.
